Short Bio

My research centers on developing advanced geospatial methods for environmental health applications, with a focus on the Bayesian Maximum Entropy (BME) data fusion framework. I specialize in integrating diverse data streams—including ground monitoring, model outputs, and satellite observations—to create high-resolution maps of hazardous air pollutants and criteria pollutants like ozone. My work spans regional studies in the US Gulf States, where I quantify exposure disparities across demographic groups, and extends to global-scale mapping projects. Through these efforts, I aim to provide critical data for health impact assessments, environmental justice analyses, and evidence-based policy making to address air pollution challenges.
