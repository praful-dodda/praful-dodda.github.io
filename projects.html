MBMEGUI: Modern BME Geostatistical Software

I developed MBMEGUI, a comprehensive software package implementing the Modern Bayesian Maximum Entropy (MBME) geostatistical framework for space/time mapping and analysis. This user-friendly GUI-based tool is actively used in environmental science courses at UNC-Chapel Hill's ESE department, providing students and researchers with accessible methods for advanced geospatial analysis. The software features an intuitive interface for data integration, mapping functions, and visualization capabilities essential for environmental modeling applications. MBMEGUI streamlines complex geostatistical processes while maintaining the rigorous mathematical foundation of the BME framework.

Hazardous Air Pollutant Exposure Mapping in the US Gulf States

This project applies the Bayesian Maximum Entropy (BME) data fusion framework to map SBTEX (Styrene, Benzene, Toluene, Ethylbenzene, Xylenes) concentrations across the US Gulf States from 2011-2016. By integrating ground monitoring data with bias-corrected CAMx model outputs and Land Use Regression offsets, we create high-resolution exposure maps for these hazardous air pollutants. Our analysis reveals long-term concentration trends and, critically, quantifies exposure disparities across demographic groups using Population Weighted Exposure Ratios. The results identify geographic areas with significant environmental justice concerns where minority populations face disproportionate exposure to these harmful pollutants.

Cancer Risk Assessment from 1,3-Butadiene Exposure

This research extends our work to 1,3-butadiene, a potent carcinogen, in the US Gulf States from 2011-2016. Using the BME framework with multiple data streams (ground observations, emissions-informed Land Use Regression, and bias-corrected CAMx model outputs), we generate high-resolution exposure estimates across the region. Beyond mapping spatial patterns and temporal trends, this project conducts a comprehensive cancer risk assessment for leukemia potentially attributable to ambient 1,3-butadiene exposure. Our analysis considers both general population exposure and potentially higher exposure scenarios for outdoor workers and those in proximity to industrial sources, providing crucial information for public health protection and regulatory decision-making.

Global High-Resolution Ground-Level Ozone Mapping

This ambitious project creates monthly global ground-level ozone concentration maps at 0.1°×0.1° resolution for 2013-2022. Our methodology implements an advanced BME data fusion framework that integrates diverse global datasets: ground observations from TOAR-II (hard data) with multiple soft data streams including satellite measurements (IASI+GOME-2), multi-model CTM ensemble outputs (M3-Fusion), and machine learning predictions. A key innovation is our application of spatially heterogeneous bias correction (RAMP method) to the soft data prior to fusion. The resulting validated 10-year global dataset provides unprecedented spatial and temporal coverage of ozone concentrations, formatted specifically for health impact assessment (global disease burden) and agricultural yield loss evaluation.
